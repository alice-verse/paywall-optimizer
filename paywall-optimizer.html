02 — The Friction

Three points where the reader gives up.

Pulled from session-recording patterns typical of card-only, form-heavy checkout on Indian mobile traffic.

Friction 01

8-field card form

Name, email, phone, billing address, card number, expiry, CVV, OTP — each additional field measurably compounds drop-off on mobile.

Friction 02

No UPI at the paywall

UPI is the default payment habit for most Indian mobile readers. Card-only checkout forces an unfamiliar, higher-friction path.

Friction 03

Ad-heavy pre-paywall load

Interstitials before the paywall slow load time and prime readers to bounce before they even see the offer.

04 — The PRD

Written up the way I'd hand it to engineering.

One-Click UPI Upsell

DRAFT · v0.3
Problem

92% of readers who hit the paywall never convert. Session data suggests the checkout form, not the offer itself, is the primary drop-off point — most acutely between checkout start and payment completion.

Goal

Replace the card-first checkout form with a single UPI-based one-tap payment at the paywall moment, for logged-in mobile readers with a verified number.

Success metric
8.0% → 9.2%
Paywall → Paid Conversion
+15%
Relative Lift
+600
Incremental Subs / Mo
Requirements
  • Detect UPI-linked apps on device; surface GPay / PhonePe / Paytm as primary CTAs above the card option.
  • Pre-fill mobile number for logged-in users; collapse the form to one visible field.
  • Fall back to the existing card flow for desktop and non-UPI users — no regression for that segment.
  • Log funnel events at each stage (paywall_view, checkout_start, payment_complete) split by payment method for post-launch analysis.
Out of scope

Redesigning the ad load before the paywall, and any change to subscription pricing tiers — tracked as separate workstreams.

Risks
  • UPI transaction failures on bank-side timeouts could look like product-side drop-off — needs separate error-state tracking, not lumped into abandonment.
  • Desktop traffic (~30% of paywall hits) sees no benefit from this change and needs its own follow-up.
